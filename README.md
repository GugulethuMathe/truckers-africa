Truckers Africa
Your Trusted Partner on the African Road
Truckers Africa is a location-aware web application designed to solve a critical problem for truck drivers traveling across Africa: finding reliable and nearby services. From mechanical repairs and border clearing agents to safe rest stops and hot meals, our platform serves as the essential bridge between drivers in need and the merchants who can serve them.

Core Features
For Truck Drivers (Free Access)
Intelligent Route Planning: Plan multi-stop trips and discover verified merchants directly along the planned route.
Geolocation-Based Discovery: The app automatically detects the driver's location to show the nearest merchants and services first.
Powerful Search & Filtering: Search for specific services (e.g., "Tyre repair") and filter results by category or rating.

Detailed Merchant Profiles: View comprehensive profiles with photos, services, opening hours, and contact information.

Ratings and Reviews: Read authentic reviews from other drivers and leave your own to help the community.
Rest Notifications: The app can notify drivers to take a break after a configurable amount of continuous driving time.

For Merchants (Subscription-Based)
Self-Service Dashboard: An intuitive portal for merchants to manage their presence on the platform.

Business Profile Management: Easily create and update their business profile with a name, description, location, contact details, and photos.
Service & Product Listings: Add, edit, and remove the specific services they offer from a predefined list.

Subscription Management: Securely subscribe to a plan to make their listing visible to thousands of drivers.
For Administrators
Merchant Verification: A system to review and approve new merchant profiles to maintain platform quality and trust.
User Management: The ability to oversee all registered drivers and merchants.
Platform Settings: A control panel to manage global settings like default search radius and maximum driving times.
Technology Stack
Framework: CodeIgniter 4
Database: MySQL
Frontend: HTML5, Tailwind CSS, Vanilla JavaScript
Dependency Management: Composer

Objective: Design a modern, intuitive, and mobile-first UI for the main dashboard of the "Truckers Africa" web application. The dashboard is for truck drivers and must be easy to use on the road, with a focus on clarity, readability, and quick access to critical information.

Core Theme & Style:

Style: Clean, modern, and professional.
Layout: A map-centric design should be the primary focus.
Color Palette: Use a high-contrast color scheme suitable for both day and night use. A base of dark greys or blues (#1F2937, #374151) with accents of a vibrant color like blue (#3B82F6) or amber (#F59E0B) for calls-to-action, routes, and important icons.
Typography: Use a clear, legible sans-serif font like Inter or Roboto.
Framework: The design should be implemented using Tailwind CSS.
Key Components & Features:

Main Dashboard View (The Map):
The central element should be a large, interactive map that displays the driver's current location with a distinct pulsating marker.
Overlay the map with icons representing different merchant categories (e.g., a wrench for mechanics, a fuel pump for gas stations, a bed for rest stops). These icons must be clickable.
Include a floating search bar at the top of the screen with the placeholder text "Search for services, e.g., 'tyre repair'".
A prominent "Plan Route" button should be easily accessible.
Route Planning Interface:
When the "Plan Route" button is clicked, a simple form should appear (e.g., in a sidebar or a modal).
The form needs fields for "Start Location," "Destination," and an option to "+ Add Stop" for multi-stop journeys.
Once a route is entered, it should be clearly drawn on the map. The merchants displayed should automatically filter to show only those relevant to the planned route.
Merchant Feed / List View:
This should be a scrollable list, perhaps as a draggable bottom sheet on mobile or a fixed sidebar on larger screens.
Each item in the list is a "Merchant Card" containing:
Merchant Name (bold)
Service Category (e.g., "Border Clearing Agent")
Star Rating (e.g., 4.5 â˜…) and number of reviews (e.g., "(12 reviews)")
Distance from the driver's current location (e.g., "5.2 km away").
A status indicator (e.g., a green dot for "Open Now").
Merchant Detail Page:
This view appears when a driver taps on a merchant's map icon or list item.
It should include:
A header with the merchant's name and a gallery of photos.
A clear list of "Services Offered."
Contact information with "Click-to-Call" and "Get Directions" buttons.
Detailed opening hours.
A section for user reviews, with a simple form for the driver to leave their own rating and comments.
Driver Profile & Notifications:
An icon (e.g., a user avatar) in the corner should lead to the driver's profile page, where they can see their saved routes and review history.
A non-intrusive toast notification should be designed for rest break reminders. For example: "Time for a break! Tap to see nearby rest stops."
